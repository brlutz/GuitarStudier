How to use guitar triads:

Triads are important as they allow you to find chord tones really quickly. This helps you learn the fretboard and be able to find notes on the fretboard when soloing over chords. This means you can now break out of the pentatonic box when soloing: simply focus on playing the notes in the triad over the related chord in your progression.

Practice Tips:

Like a lot of intermediate guitar pratice tips, there isn't a lot of magic in this, just practice. Being a great guitarist requires knowing the fretboard at the same level you understand what word to use when you're speaking, or what key to hit on the keyboard when typing, or what button to hit on a controller when playing a game.

Set yourself a goal in exercises or time. Something like 50 triads or 30 minutes a day. Set yourself a schedule, get some free time and practice without distractions. You'll start slow at first, but slowly, with practice, will increase the speed you're able to play these. Getting fast will take practice, but that's all it takes! Practice consistency and you'll see consistent results.
